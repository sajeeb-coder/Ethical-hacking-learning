Junior Security Analyst Intro (TryHackMe)

This room introduced me to the role and responsibilities of a Junior
 Security Analyst within a Security Operations Center (SOC). It provided a
 hands-on experience of what daily tasks and challenges a SOC analyst
 faces, including monitoring, triaging alerts, and understanding security
 incidents in a controlled environment.

Key Concepts & Tools Covered

SIEM Dashboard
Learned how to inspect critical alerts, identify malicious activity, and
 understand the workflow of alert management.

Alert Triage
Practiced reviewing security alerts and escalating genuine threats to
 higher-tier analysts, emphasizing prioritization.

SOC Team Roles
Understood the responsibilities of different SOC team members, including
 Tier 1 analysts, Tier 2 analysts, and incident responders.

Malicious IP Investigation
Practiced identifying suspicious IP addresses, understanding the impact,
 and reporting procedures.

Incident Response Workflow
Gained a high-level understanding of how a SOC responds to incidents,
 including analysis, documentation, and communication.

Ethical & Legal Considerations
Reinforced that all activities must remain within legal and ethical
 boundaries.

What I Learned

The daily workflow of a SOC analyst and how alerts are monitored and handled

How to identify critical vs. false-positive alerts

The importance of teamwork and clear communication in a SOC environment

How security tools assist analysts, but human judgment is essential for
 prioritizing incidents

This room provided a practical foundation for understanding the Blue Team
 perspective, which complements offensive security skills and strengthens
 overall cybersecurity knowledge.
